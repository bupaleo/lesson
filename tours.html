Spend your summer at Issyk-Kul!

Issyk-Kul with its crystal clear water, mineral springs, magnificent sandy beaches and combination of sea and mountain climate is an excellent resort area with mud and thermo-mineral treatment. Healing water of springs and the lake itself is used in swimming pools, and the therapeutic sulfide silt mud in combination with natural and climatic factors of Issyk-Kul is used to treat many chronic diseases.

Skiing in Karakol

Very close to Karakol you will find beautiful mountain landscapes with snowy peaks and spectacular views of waterfalls, lakes and canyons with red rocks. All this makes Karakol an ideal place from which you can easily organize day trips to immerse yourself in nature and adventure during the day and return to the city in the evening.

Jailoo Sarala-Saz

Zhailoo Sarala-Saz is a high mountain pasture located 54 km northwest of Kochkor village. It is located at an altitude of over 3,000 m. Jailoo got its name, which means "yellowish swamp" in Kyrgyz, because of the characteristic color of the grass growing here and the virtual absence of bushes and trees around.

Kol Tor Lake

The Kel-Tor tract is a small branch of the Kegeti Gorge, which is remarkable for its beautiful mountain lake, located at an altitude of 2,700 meters. The lake is of rubble origin, has no direct flow and goes underground, bursting out of the ground a few kilometers below. Most of the year the lake is frozen, and in the summer the water never gets warmer than 5 degrees Celsius, so it is not suitable for swimming.

Kel-Suu Lake

Kel-Suu is a lake that originates in high mountain glaciers. It is located in the southeast of Naryn province, near the Kyrgyz-Chinese border, at an altitude of 3,500 meters. The lake is incredibly beautiful and picturesque. Its water is bright blue and blends beautifully with the surrounding rocks. It is one of the most beautiful and at the same time the most difficult places in Kyrgyzstan. The lake has a distinctive feature. Interestingly, at some point the lake disappears and flows into underground caves and hollows.

Burana Tower

Burana Tower (aka Burana Minaret) is rightfully considered a cultural heritage of Kyrgyzstan. The attraction is located among the ruins of an ancient settlement, ten minutes from the town of Tokmak on the left bank of the small river Chu. The structure was built in the X-XI centuries. Near the tower is a "rock garden", which includes balbals - ancient stone sculptures of the VI-X centuries, ancient millstones and samples of rock art I century BC.

Son-Kul Lake

Son-Kul Lake (also Song-Kul, Son-Kyol, Sonkul, etc.) is a large mountain lake, the second largest in Kyrgyzstan, which is located in Naryn province, about 280 kilometers from Bishkek. Son-Kul is located at an altitude of 3,000 meters in a large intermountain valley, bounded by the Sonkul-Too ridge to the north and the Moldo-Too ridge to the south. The lake is fresh and quite deep - the maximum depth of the lake is 14 meters. Around the middle of November, Son-Kul freezes and thaws only by the middle of April.
